Most people evaluate decisions by weighing the probability of success. But Sheel argues the real question is: what's the shape of the payoff? An asymmetric bet is one where you can't lose much, but you can gain enormously. The math changes when you realize most of the best outcomes in life come from bets where the downside was trivially small compared to the upside. Stop optimizing for probability. Start optimizing for payoff shape.
